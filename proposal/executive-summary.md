# Executive Summary

## Understanding of Meridian's Needs

Meridian Components requires modernization and extension of their existing inventory management dashboard to address operational inefficiencies and incomplete functionality from the previous vendor engagement. The current Vue.js/Python system serves three global warehouses but suffers from unresolved defects in the Reports module, lacks automated testing infrastructure, and misses critical capabilities like intelligent restocking recommendations.

## Proposed Approach

We will deliver a comprehensive modernization that prioritizes operational impact while establishing sustainable development practices. Our approach focuses on:

- **Immediate remediation** of known Reports module defects to restore dashboard reliability
- **Strategic enhancement** with a new Restocking view that leverages demand forecasting and budget constraints
- **Quality assurance foundation** through automated end-to-end testing to enable safe future evolution
- **Knowledge transfer** via detailed architecture documentation for Meridian IT

## Key Differentiators

- **Operations-first perspective**: Direct engagement with VP Tanaka's team to ensure features solve real workflow pain points
- **Risk mitigation**: Comprehensive testing strategy to address IT's change management concerns
- **Global readiness**: Enhanced internationalization to support Tokyo warehouse operations
- **Predictable delivery**: Phased approach with clear milestones and quality gates

## Timeline and Investment

We propose a 6-week delivery timeline with weekly checkpoints, enabling Meridian to realize value incrementally while maintaining operational continuity. Our fixed-price approach provides budget certainty for this critical modernization initiative.

This proposal represents our commitment to transforming Meridian's inventory dashboard from a functional but limited system into a robust, extensible platform that supports continued growth across San Francisco, London, and Tokyo operations.</content>
